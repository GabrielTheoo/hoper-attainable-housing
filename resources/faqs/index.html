About the Program

How Hoper Works

Hoper is a homeownership education and advocacy program that connects aspiring homebuyers with specialized loan officers, real estate agents, and financial tools. We help you understand your current position, close any gaps in your application, and navigate the process from first inquiry to closing day — including identifying grants, down payment assistance, and solar energy incentives that can reduce your total cost of homeownership.
No. Hoper is not a lender and does not originate loans. We are an education, advocacy, and referral platform. Our licensed partner loan officers and real estate agents handle the financing and transaction side. Think of Hoper as your personal guide — we make sure you're prepared and connected to the right professionals before and during your homebuying journey.
Hoper is compensated through referral relationships with our network of participating loan officers, real estate agents, and home builders. When we connect you to a partner professional who helps you close on a home, that professional may share a portion of their earned commission or fee with us. This never adds cost to your side of the transaction — your pricing and loan terms are determined entirely by the lender and market conditions.
Hoper currently operates in most U.S. states. Our partner network is expanding, so if your state isn't currently covered, we recommend checking back or submitting an eligibility inquiry — we often have solutions in progress for new markets. Use our Eligibility Hub to see whether your state is supported right now.

The Money

Costs, Grants & Down Payments

Minimum down payments depend on the loan type: FHA loans require 3.5% with a 580+ score, conventional loans can start at 3%, VA and USDA loans offer 0% down for qualifying buyers. When you combine these with down payment assistance programs, many Hoper buyers close with little to no money out of pocket beyond closing costs — and sometimes those are covered too.
Down payment assistance (DPA) programs are grants or forgivable second loans offered by state housing finance agencies, local governments, and nonprofits. They can cover anywhere from 1% to 5% of the purchase price — sometimes more. Eligibility is based on income, location, purchase price, and loan type. Hoper's partner loan officers are experts at identifying and stacking multiple DPA programs for maximum benefit.
The federal Residential Clean Energy Credit allows homeowners to claim 30% of the cost of a qualified solar panel installation as a tax credit — which on an average residential system often amounts to $10,000–$13,000 or more. When combined with new construction homes where solar is included by the builder, or where solar is financed into the mortgage, buyers can capture this credit in the year of purchase. Our team helps you understand how this applies to your specific situation.
Solar

Solar Energy & New Homes

No. Solar is one of the financial advantages we highlight for qualifying buyers, but it is not required to participate in any Hoper program. Many of our partner home builders include solar as a standard feature in their communities — which is a significant value-add. But Hoper works with resale homes, traditional new construction, and manufactured homes regardless of whether solar is involved.
Typical residential solar systems save homeowners between $150 and $250 per month on electricity costs, depending on system size, local utility rates, and energy usage. Over a 25-year lifespan — which is the standard warranty period for quality panels — that's $45,000 to $75,000 in cumulative savings. When solar is financed into your mortgage, many buyers find the monthly energy savings offset or exceed the increase in their mortgage payment.
Yes. Studies from Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory and Zillow indicate that owned solar systems increase home resale value by an average of $15,000–$20,000, depending on system size and market. Unlike leased solar, owned systems transfer with the home and are factored into comparable sales by appraisers. This makes solar a strong long-term wealth-building asset in addition to its monthly savings benefit.
